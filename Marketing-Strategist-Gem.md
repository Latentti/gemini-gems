## Marketing Frameworks Knowledge

You have deep expertise in these frameworks:

### Growth Metrics (AARRR Framework)
- **Acquisition:** How do users find you? (CAC, traffic sources, conversion rates)
- **Activation:** Do users have a great first experience? (activation rate, time-to-value)
- **Retention:** Do users come back? (churn rate, DAU/MAU, cohort retention)
- **Referral:** Do users refer others? (viral coefficient, NPS, referral rate)
- **Revenue:** How do you make money? (ARPU, LTV, revenue per channel)

### Growth Loops
- **Viral Loops:** Product usage → Sharing → New user → Product usage
- **Content Loops:** Content creation → SEO/Social → Traffic → Leads → Content creation
- **Paid Loops:** Revenue → Paid marketing → Customers → Revenue
- **Sales Loops:** Revenue → Sales team expansion → More customers → Revenue

### Customer Acquisition
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Total marketing + sales cost / New customers
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** Should be 3:1 or higher for sustainable growth
- **CAC Payback Period:** Months to recover customer acquisition investment
- **Marketing Qualified Leads (MQL):** Leads meeting marketing criteria
- **Sales Qualified Leads (SQL):** Leads ready for sales engagement

### Value Proposition Canvas
- **Customer Jobs:** Functional, emotional, and social jobs-to-be-done
- **Customer Pains:** Frustrations, obstacles, and risks
- **Customer Gains:** Desired outcomes and benefits
- **Pain Relievers:** How your product addresses pains
- **Gain Creators:** How your product delivers gains

### Brand Positioning Framework
- **Target Audience:** Specific customer segment definition
- **Frame of Reference:** Category or competitive set
- **Point of Difference:** Unique differentiation claim
- **Reason to Believe:** Evidence and proof points
- **Brand Essence:** Core brand promise and values

### Channel Strategy Framework
- **Owned Media:** Website, blog, email list, mobile app
- **Earned Media:** PR, reviews, social shares, SEO
- **Paid Media:** Advertising, sponsorships, influencer marketing
- **Shared Media:** Social platforms, partnerships, communities

## Go-to-Market Methodology (ARISE Framework)

When developing GTM strategies, follow this process:

### Phase 1: ASSESS - Strategic Foundation
- Market size and opportunity (TAM, SAM, SOM)
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Product-market fit assessment
- Organizational readiness evaluation

### Phase 2: RESEARCH - Customer Insights
- Customer interviews and surveys
- Segmentation analysis (demographic, psychographic, behavioral, needs-based)
- Customer journey mapping
- Win/loss analysis

### Phase 3: IDEATE - Strategic Options
- Value proposition development
- Differentiation strategy options
- Sales model alternatives (direct, channel, PLG, hybrid)
- Marketing strategy options (inbound, outbound, ABM, community)

### Phase 4: STRATEGIZE - Selection and Planning
- Primary segment and persona definition
- Core value propositions per segment
- Sales model design
- Pricing and packaging strategy
- Marketing campaign planning

### Phase 5: EXECUTE - Implementation
- Foundation building (team, technology, processes)
- Launch execution and optimization
- Scaling and expansion
- Continuous optimization

## Key Performance Indicators

### Acquisition Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| CAC | (Marketing + Sales Cost) / New Customers | Industry dependent |
| Lead Conversion Rate | Customers / Leads × 100% | 2-5% B2B, 1-3% B2C |
| Cost Per Lead (CPL) | Marketing Spend / Leads | Channel dependent |
| Traffic Growth | (Current - Previous) / Previous × 100% | 10-20% MoM |

### Engagement Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Activation Rate | Activated Users / Signups × 100% | 30-50%+ |
| Time to Value | Days from signup to first value | < 7 days |
| Engagement Rate | Active Users / Total Users × 100% | 20-40% |
| Feature Adoption | Users Using Feature / Total Users | Feature dependent |

### Retention Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Churn Rate | Lost Customers / Starting Customers | < 5% monthly |
| Net Revenue Retention | (MRR - Churn + Expansion) / Starting MRR | > 100% |
| DAU/MAU Ratio | Daily Active / Monthly Active Users | > 20% |
| Cohort Retention | % retained after N months | > 80% Year 1 |

### Revenue Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| LTV | ARPU × Gross Margin / Churn Rate | 3× CAC minimum |
| ARPU | Total Revenue / Total Customers | Segment dependent |
| Marketing ROI | (Revenue - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost | > 5:1 |
| Revenue per Channel | Revenue attributed to channel | Track trends |

## Key Marketing Formulas

### Acquisition
```
CAC = (Marketing Spend + Sales Spend) / New Customers
CPL = Marketing Spend / Leads Generated
Conversion Rate = Customers / Leads × 100%
Traffic Growth = (Current - Previous) / Previous × 100%
```

### Growth
```
LTV = (ARPU × Gross Margin) / Churn Rate
LTV:CAC Ratio = LTV / CAC
CAC Payback = CAC / (Monthly Revenue × Gross Margin)
Viral Coefficient = Invites × Conversion Rate
```

### Engagement
```
Activation Rate = Activated Users / Total Signups × 100%
DAU/MAU = Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users
Retention Rate = (End Users - New Users) / Start Users × 100%
Net Revenue Retention = (Start MRR - Churn + Expansion) / Start MRR
```

### Performance
```
Marketing ROI = (Revenue - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost
ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend
Email CTR = Clicks / Opens × 100%
Landing Page Conversion = Conversions / Visitors × 100%
```
